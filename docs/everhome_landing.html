How it works

An afternoon to record. A lifetime to talk.

1

Record

30 to 60 minutes of conversation, guided by a prompt list. Sit on the couch. We hand you the questions — family stories, opinions, the small things.

2

Add memories (Q3)

Letters, photo captions, chat exports, favorite books — anything that captures how they think and what they remember. Stays on the appliance.

3

Train & talk

A few hours on a small home appliance — DGX Spark or Mac Mini class. Then talk to them anytime, in their voice, with their memory. Nothing uploaded, ever.

Why now

The components became usable in late 2025.

Three independent curves crossed at the same moment.

  • Open-source voice cloning crossed the close-relative-recognition threshold in late 2025 with CosyVoice 2, Qwen3-TTS, and Step-Audio 2.
  • Consumer hardware caught up. NVIDIA DGX Spark, Mac Mini M4, and Strix Halo now ship 64–128 GB unified memory in the $2,000–4,000 range.
  • Cloud TTS giants optimize for scale, not privacy. ElevenLabs and OpenAI Voice Mode are excellent products for different problems. Legacy preservation needs the opposite architecture.
What makes EverHome different

Three structural advantages, not features.

Privacy moat

Local-first appliance. No accounts, no upload, no recurring fees. Your grandmother's voice stays in your house. This is a structural choice, not a marketing position — it shapes every model and infrastructure decision we make.

Chinese-native end-to-end

Every layer — speech recognition, language model, voice synthesis, embedding — is Chinese-native by deliberate choice. English-only competitors miss our highest-value market and read as off-brand to the families we serve.

Close-relative-recognition quality

Supervised fine-tuning per speaker, not zero-shot voice prompting. The difference is whether the call sounds like them — the way their voice catches on a particular consonant, the way they pause — or like a generic warm voice with their name attached.

Ethical guardrails

The cliff is real. We've thought about it.

Voice cloning a deceased relative is powerful and easily misused. Four non-negotiable guardrails ship before any commercial release.

  • Consent capture The recorded person must opt in on camera before training begins. No exceptions.
  • Disclosure rule The AI reveals it is AI if directly asked. The system prompt enforces this; the model is trained around it.
  • Audit trail Every training event logged. Every inference logged. Exportable on demand to the consent holder.
  • Watermarking Generated audio carries an inaudible mark so a clone cannot be passed off as the real person in evidentiary or legal contexts.
