The problem

Voices and stories disappear.

Families lose access to grandparents' voices, stories, and ways of speaking — sometimes all at once, more often a year at a time. Three failure modes, all permanent.

Dementia

The person is here. Their stories are not. The window closes earlier than families expect, and there is no second recording session.

Distance

Two generations of immigration, work, and time zones mean grandchildren grow up with the person on a screen for twenty minutes once a month, in English.

Death

What we have left is photographs and a few voicemails. No way to ask one more question. No way to hear them say our name again.

Cloud-AI alternatives exist, but they require sending your grandmother's voice to a third party. That is the reason EverHome exists.

The solution

30 to 60 minutes. Then forever.

EverHome turns recorded conversation into a voice AI clone that your family can talk to forever, on hardware you own.

  • A cloned voice close relatives recognize. Supervised fine-tuning per speaker, not a generic warm voice with their name printed on it.
  • Memory of who they were. Stories, opinions, jokes, ways of speaking. Built from recorded conversation, photographs, letters, and books.
  • Runs on a small box in your home. Nothing leaves the house. No accounts, no upload, no recurring fees.
How it works

Three steps. One afternoon to start.

1

Record

30 to 60 minutes of conversation, guided by a prompt list. Sit on the couch. We hand you the questions.

2

Train

A few hours on a small home appliance — DGX Spark or Mac Mini class. Fully local. Nothing uploaded.

3

Talk

Speak to them anytime. The voice clones their timbre; the memory captures who they were.

Why now

The components became usable in late 2025.

Three independent curves crossed at the same moment.

  • Open-source voice cloning crossed the close-relative-recognition threshold in late 2025 with CosyVoice 2, Qwen3-TTS, and Step-Audio 2.
  • Consumer hardware caught up. NVIDIA DGX Spark, Mac Mini M4, and Strix Halo now ship 64–128 GB unified memory in the $2,000–4,000 range.
  • Cloud TTS giants optimize for scale, not privacy. ElevenLabs and OpenAI Voice Mode are excellent products for different problems. Legacy preservation needs the opposite architecture.
What makes EverHome different

Three structural advantages, not features.

Privacy moat

Local-first appliance. No accounts, no upload, no recurring fees. Your grandmother's voice stays in your house. This is a structural choice, not a marketing position — it shapes every model and infrastructure decision we make.

Chinese-native end-to-end

Every layer — speech recognition, language model, voice synthesis, embedding — is Chinese-native by deliberate choice. English-only competitors miss our highest-value market and read as off-brand to the families we serve.

Close-relative-recognition quality

Supervised fine-tuning per speaker, not zero-shot voice prompting. The difference is whether the call sounds like them — the way their voice catches on a particular consonant, the way they pause — or like a generic warm voice with their name attached.

Markets

Two paths. Same product.

B2C — direct to families

Families who want to preserve a grandparent, parent, or partner. One-time hardware purchase plus a small annual maintenance fee. No subscription tier, no usage-based billing, no "premium voices."

B2B2C — partners

Eldercare facilities, hospice partners, memorial-service SaaS, and Chinese-diaspora community organizations. White-label or co-branded deployments, each with a per-deployment persona specification and consent workflow.

Ethical guardrails

The cliff is real. We've thought about it.

Voice cloning a deceased relative is powerful and easily misused. Four non-negotiable guardrails ship before any commercial release.

  • Consent capture The recorded person must opt in on camera before training begins. No exceptions.
  • Disclosure rule The AI reveals it is AI if directly asked. The system prompt enforces this; the model is trained around it.
  • Audit trail Every training event logged. Every inference logged. Exportable on demand to the consent holder.
  • Watermarking Generated audio carries an inaudible mark so a clone cannot be passed off as the real person in evidentiary or legal contexts.
