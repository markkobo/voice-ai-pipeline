A. Directional surprises (things likely to bite)
- Dataset/license landmines in M7/M9: PersonaHub (Tencent) and many HF “OpenCharacter” clones ship under CC BY-NC/NC-like terms; you can’t legally use NC data to train a commercial persona LoRA. Treat PersonaHub as off-limits until the repo LICENSE explicitly allows commercial use; prefer generating your own synthetic persona corpora via the Open Character Training pipeline. Also double-check MinerU 2.5-Pro “Pro” artifacts and emotion2vec model cards for commercial-use clauses before baking them into the box (licenses drift across ModelScope/HF repos).
- Qwen supply risk and tokenizers: the Qwen family has split repos and differing licenses across subprojects; some Qwen audio repos have moved or changed licenses in the past. If Alibaba pivots product lines, TTS or ASR repos can be deprecated quietly. Mitigation: watch QwenLM GitHub/HF release RSS, pin exact commits, mirror weights internally, and have CosyVoice 2 and VoxCPM 2 as warm backups behind BaseTTSEngine. Keep tracking Qwen-Omni codec/tokenizer access; closed codecs have already blocked SFT in practice (HF Qwen2.5-Omni-7B discussion #40).
- Browser audio capture will break in production Safari/Chrome: ScriptProcessorNode/onaudioprocess is long-deprecated; Safari’s AudioWorklet input support is picky. Replace input capture with an AudioWorkletProcessor + SharedArrayBuffer path and test mic permissions on iOS/iPadOS early (MDN marks ScriptProcessorNode deprecated since 2020). Your “listen-only” demo path could regress on mobile if you keep onaudioprocess.
- Consent scope for chat exports/photos: WhatsApp/WeChat/LINE exports include third parties who did not consent to model training. Under NO FAKES/EU AI Act/CCPA you’ll need per-sender consent or robust redaction. Plan an ingestion-time “third-party content filter” and default-exclude non-speaker voices/photos unless consent is recorded; otherwise revocations will cascade into unlearning requirements you can’t meet quickly.
- Watermarking expectations vs reality: many audio watermarks are brittle to compression/mixing. If you advertise watermarking for compliance, pick a specific, tested scheme and publish constraints. Evaluate Meta’s AudioSeal (2024) and WavMark-style spread-spectrum schemes; measure detectability after MP3 64–128 kbps, room playback/re-record, and 10–20% timestretch. Regulators increasingly expect provenance that survives common transforms.

B. Sequencing errors (what to move earlier/later)
- Run M12a before M12, not after: your top-level list still shows M12 preceding M12a in places. The zero/one-day spikes (Step-Audio 2 mini, Qwen3.5-Omni-Light) should gate any Omni-hybrid investment. Do M11 → M12a → decide M12 base (Omni/Kimi/LLaMA-Omni) or defer.
- Move consent gating in front of ingestion: M-Consent should sit before users can upload large corpora (parallel engineering is fine, but gate the UI so new personas can’t be ingested without a consent record). Otherwise you’ll accumulate training data you may not be allowed to process, creating retroactive unlearning work.
- Pull “real talker.model LoRA” forward: make the talker-side LoRA investigation a blocking subtask of M8.5 Week 0 (or attach to M11). Instruction-conditioning without a deeper talker LoRA will continue to drift under emotion control; you already saw speaker instability with per-sentence instruct.
- Thin-slice memory earlier: ID-RAG + HippoRAG 2 is ambitious; ship an “M8a minimal memory” in 3–5 days (BGE-M3 + LanceDB + transparent citations) before the full identity graph/conversation-memory stack. This derisks integration into ws_asr.py and gives you a quick retrieval latency baseline while ID-graph work runs.
- Baseline eval harnesses sooner: add CharacterEval and your family A/B “close-relative recognition” harness as prework to M8.5/M9, not accepts-only. You’ll need these to compare TTS retrains and any S2S spikes apples-to-apples.

C. Moat thesis stress-test (what could kill it in 6–12 months)
- Open-weight TTS with easy speaker SFT: VoxCPM 2 (OpenBMB/Tsinghua, late-2025/2026) and CosyVoice 2/3 are advancing fast; if either ships a robust per-speaker LoRA/SFT recipe with 30–60 min audio and decent Chinese cross-lingual prosody, your “depth via Qwen3-TTS-LoRA” stops being unique. Keep VoxCPM 2 and CosyVoice 2 as immediate BaseTTSEngine targets and measure your family-recognition A/B against them.
- Qwen3.5-Omni (or successor) open-sourcing Talker with SFT: if Alibaba releases weights/tokenizer or a sanctioned cloning head, your SFT-only moat collapses to training data + product fit. The project already cites arXiv:2604.15804; assume a 6–12 month window for a partial open that’s “good enough.”
- Big-platform “legacy voice” features: Apple/Google/Huawei could bundle high-quality on-device cloning + memory with “Legacy Contact” UX. Apple’s Personal Voice shipped in 2023; an LLM-grounded, on-device successor would vacuum up B2C diaspora demand. Your B2B hospice/eldercare channel mitigates this risk—lean into that now.
- Compliance shocks: if EU/US jurisdictions mandate audible disclosures each utterance (or ban post-mortem replicas without a court order), audible watermarking will torpedo illusion. Track EU AI Act delegated acts and US state bills beyond CA (NY/TX deepfake statutes are in flux).
- Data-network effects: a competitor signs national funeral/memorial chains and amasses a million-voice corpus (plus 10k persona RLHF like Step-Audio 2.5 Realtime). Roleplay-specific RLHF on top of zero-shot cloning halves your current perceived advantage even without SFT.

D. Things missing entirely (worth adding)
- Unlearning plan and SLAs: revocation requires model rebuilds; specify an “unlearning” design (e.g., SISA training shards for TTS LoRA; track per-sample contribution and allow shard drop-and-retrain) and publish an SLA (“revoked segment removed from synthesized output within X days”). Cite SISA Training (USENIX Security 2019) as the pattern.
- Security/DR for an on-prem grief product: full-disk encryption, encrypted backups, recovery keys for deceased-person estates, and a one-click export/import between boxes. A dead disk without off-box encrypted backup is catastrophic for families.
- Legal review + DPA templates: Data Processing Addendum (GDPR), standard consent language localized (US/EU/TW/HK), and a documented stance on biometric data (voice is biometric in some regimes). Add DSAR/erasure flows to the UI.
- Model/weight registry and reproducibility: a simple MLMD (or just JSON + SHA256) registry for every trained artifact (base commit, data snapshot, hyperparams, random seeds). You’ll need this for audit/discovery and for reconstructing models post-revocation.
- Front-end capture hardening and platform coverage: implement input via AudioWorkletProcessor + SharedArrayBuffer; test on Safari/iOS and WeChat in-app browser (which changes autoplay/mic policies). Add TURN servers for bad NATs; Cloudflare tunnels alone won’t fix WebRTC-hostile networks if you move there.
- Release tracking automation: implement the watch script you sketched (HF/arXiv/GitHub polling) and a monthly “go/no-go” note. Add VoxCPM, CosyVoice, GLM-4-Voice, Kimi-Audio, Qwen3.5-Omni to the watch set with license/recipe fields.

E. Decision points to re-examine (given 2026 landscape)
- M12 base choice: don’t default to Qwen2.5-Omni-7B. Run a 1-week bake-off including Kimi-Audio-7B and LLaMA-Omni 2 as hybrid cores; your Qwen 3 8B persona LoRA may transfer more cleanly to those Qwen 2.5–based stacks, and their ASR quality on Traditional Chinese may be better than Omni-7B.
- TTS backend priority: add VoxCPM 2 as a first-class M11 target alongside IndexTTS-2. Its tokenizer-free design (48 kHz) directly addresses your “telephone-grade” sound and language-leak gotcha; if it matches cloning depth with LoRA, you can retire Qwen3-TTS sooner.
- Embeddings and RAG plumbing: keep BGE-M3 as default, but plan an A/B against GTE-Qwen2/multilingual-e5-large for mixed-script corpora; add an episodic store (timestamps, speakers) alongside your semantic indices. HippoRAG 2 for stance is right; validate with LoCoMo-style metrics.
- Watermarking tech choice: pick and publish one scheme (e.g., Meta AudioSeal 2024 with your own keyed implementation) and document its limits; don’t ship a checkbox that implies legal sufficiency. Consider dual-mode: inaudible provenance + optional audible disclosure for consumer markets.
- Data-source policy: explicitly exclude non-consenting third-party chat/photo content at ingest by default. If you intend to use PersonaHub/OpenCharacter datasets, audit their licenses now; if NC, generate synthetic in-house via Open Character Training instead of touching NC corpora.
